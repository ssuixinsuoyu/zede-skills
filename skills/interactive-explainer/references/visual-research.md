# Online Visual Research

Research real design work before proposing a visual direction. Use the internet to replace vague taste words with observable design decisions.

## Source priority

Prefer original project pages and studio portfolios. Useful starting points include:

- [Pentagram Work](https://www.pentagram.com/work/) for editorial systems, typography, data visualization, identity, and motion.
- [Pentagram Editorial Design](https://www.pentagram.com/editorial-design) for page rhythm, theme-and-variation, and image/type balance.
- [Studio Dumbar Work](https://studiodumbar.com/work) for identity systems driven by typography and motion.
- [BUCK Work](https://buck.co/work) for art-directed illustration, character, visual systems, and motion craft.
- [Ordinary Folk](https://www.ordinaryfolk.co/) for clear, expressive explainer motion and restrained geometric storytelling.
- [DIA](https://www.dia.studio/) for kinetic identity systems and type-led motion.

Use award galleries or editorial roundups only to discover work. Trace a reference back to the original studio or project page before relying on it. Avoid Pinterest boards, template marketplaces, SEO galleries, and unattributed reposts as primary references.

## Research procedure

1. Form 2-4 searches from the script's topic plus information structure, such as `robotics visual identity motion system`, `editorial archaeology publication design`, or `learning process kinetic infographic`.
2. Inspect at least six plausible examples across at least three independent original sources.
3. Keep 2-4 references that are structurally useful for a 16:9 explanation frame.
4. Record one transferable principle from each reference:
   - grid and spatial tension;
   - type scale and line length;
   - palette restraint;
   - image crop or illustration treatment;
   - material and texture;
   - motion timing and transformation.
5. Link the direct project pages in the style shortlist.

## Synthesis rules

- Combine principles, not branded surface details.
- Never reproduce a logo, custom mark, character, branded color combination, signature illustration, or near-identical composition.
- Translate a reference into content-specific rules for the current script.
- Prefer one strong visual idea over a collection of fashionable effects.
- If live research is unavailable, say so and use the built-in style system as a fallback; do not claim that references were checked.

## Anti-AI taste filter

Reject a direction when it depends on any of these defaults without a content reason:

- purple-blue gradient, neon orb, random glow, or floating glass panels;
- centered headline followed by three equal rounded cards;
- generic bento grid, dashboard, fake app interface, or fake code window;
- emoji, stock 3D icons, isometric AI networks, floating cubes, or decorative circuit lines;
- every element rounded, softly shadowed, and equally important;
- many accent colors, random blobs, sparkles, or grain added only to look designed;
- the same composition repeated on every page;
- imagery that could belong to any AI, productivity, or technology presentation.

Do not ban a technique categorically. Use it only when the script and cited references provide a clear semantic reason.
